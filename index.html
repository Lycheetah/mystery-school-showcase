The Engine

One world. Many eyes.

Under the RPG world runs a pixel engine of its own making. The world is simulated in world-space and doesn't know how it's drawn — so camera, light and collision are held apart from the simulation, and the same living region can be seen three different ways.

◈

Three cameras, one world ●Live

Isometric, top-down bird's-eye and a first-person street view — switched live and remembered between sessions.

⊚

The sim is camera-blind ●Live

Determinism, collision and light survive every eye. Changing the view never changes the world.

▲

The asset seam ●Live

Real hand-made pixel art reaches the engine on a fixed palette; the atlas exports byte-for-byte, deterministically.

↺

Replayable by design ●Live

The world can be snapshotted and replayed exactly — the same inputs always yield the same world.

⬡

A Rust kernel spine ◐Foundation

The deterministic core is being rebuilt in Rust, checked bit-for-bit against the live engine by a parity oracle. The MMO-shaped future.

△

Owned, editable places ◐Foundation

A furniture kernel and a Worldsmith editor let rooms be built and kept, with tools you earn — persistence still being hardened.

Lands stand in the world today — the Hearth Valley, the Mystery School Commons, the Proving Ground, and a personal Wayfarer house with connected interiors, joined by smooth tap-to-move, collision-aware approaches and portals. This is a large, handcrafted prototype; smoothness is judged on a real Android phone, never asserted, and Android rendering on big maps is the known risk.
